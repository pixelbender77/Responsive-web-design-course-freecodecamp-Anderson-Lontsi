Ella Fitzgerald

The first lady of Jazz

Here is the time line of Ella fitzgerald's life:

  • April 25, 1917: Ella Jane Fitzgerald is born in Newport News, Virginia, USA.
  • 1934 At the age of 17, Ella participates in an amateur contest at the Apollo Theater in Harlem, New York. She intends to dance but decides to sing at the last moment. This marks the beginning of her singing career.
  • 1938 Ella records her first hit, "A-Tisket, A-Tasket," with the Chick Webb Orchestra. The song becomes a major success and helps establish her as a leading jazz vocalist.
  • 1942 Following the death of Chick Webb, Ella takes over as the bandleader of the Chick Webb Orchestra.
  • 1956 Ella Fitzgerald begins a series of collaborations with jazz pianist and composer Duke Ellington, resulting in the critically acclaimed album "Ella Fitzgerald Sings the Duke Ellington Song Book
  • 1958 She releases her famous "Ella Fitzgerald Sings the Irving Berlin Songbook," one of several celebrated songbook albums.
  • 1958 Ella makes history by becoming the first African-American woman to win a Grammy Award, winning in the category of Best Jazz Performance, Individual.
  • 1960s-1970s Fitzgerald continues to record and perform extensively, earning numerous awards and accolades throughout her career.
  • 1979 She is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President George H.W. Bush.
  • 1996 Ella Fitzgerald passes away on June 15, 1996, in Beverly Hills, California, leaving behind an unparalleled legacy in jazz music.
